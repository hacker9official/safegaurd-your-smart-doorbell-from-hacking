How to Safeguard Your Smart Doorbell from Hacking: A Simple Guide

With more and more households using smart devices, securing your smart doorbell has become just as important as locking your front door. A compromised smart doorbell can give hackers access to your home security system and even your personal data. 

Let’s dive into how you can protect your smart doorbell from being hacked.

Why Smart Doorbell Security Matters

Smart doorbells are incredibly convenient, offering live video streams, two-way audio, and remote access to your home. They help keep an eye on deliveries, visitors, and intruders. But like any device connected to the internet, they can also be vulnerable to hacking.

Statistics show that nearly 50% of smart home devices, including smart doorbells, have security flaws. Hackers can exploit these vulnerabilities to gain unauthorized access, putting your privacy and security at risk. That’s why it's crucial to understand how to secure your smart doorbell and prevent any potential attacks.

Common Risks to Smart Doorbells

Before we dive into the protection steps, let’s first understand the common security risks associated with smart doorbells:

  • Weak or Default Passwords: Many people still use easy-to-guess passwords like “123456” or rely on default passwords set by manufacturers.
  • Unprotected Wi-Fi Networks: A doorbell connected to an unsecured Wi-Fi network is a prime target for hackers.
  • Outdated Software/Firmware: Regular updates are essential for fixing vulnerabilities, but many users forget to update their devices.
  • Lack of Encryption: If your doorbell's video feed and data aren't encrypted, hackers can easily intercept the signal.

Understanding these risks is the first step toward preventing an attack.

Steps to Secure Your Smart Doorbell

Now that we’ve identified the risks, let’s take a look at how you can secure your smart doorbell in just a few simple steps.

Update Your Firmware Regularly

Just like any other device, your smart doorbell needs regular updates to fix security vulnerabilities. Set a reminder to check for updates every few months, or enable automatic updates if available.

Use Strong, Unique Passwords

One of the easiest ways to secure your doorbell is by setting a strong password. Avoid using common phrases or easily guessable information. Instead, create a unique password that includes a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security. Even if a hacker manages to guess your password, they won’t be able to access your doorbell without the second factor (usually a one-time code sent to your phone). Enable 2FA in your doorbell’s app settings.

Secure Your Wi-Fi Network

Your smart doorbell depends on your Wi-Fi network, so ensuring it’s secure is crucial. Use WPA3 encryption for your Wi-Fi, and consider setting up a separate network for all your smart devices. This way, even if one device gets compromised, your main network remains safe.

Monitor Your Doorbell’s Activity

Most smart doorbells let you view logs of activity. Set up notifications to alert you whenever someone accesses your doorbell’s settings or login attempts are made. This can help you stay ahead of any suspicious behavior.

Use Encryption for Video Feeds

Ensure your video feed is encrypted to protect it from being intercepted. Encryption prevents hackers from accessing your footage, even if they manage to breach the network.

Disable Unnecessary Features

Some smart doorbells have extra features, like remote viewing or cloud storage. If you don’t use them, it’s best to disable them to reduce the potential attack surface.

What to Do if Your Smart Doorbell Gets Hacked

If you think your smart doorbell has been compromised, don’t panic. Here’s what to do:

Reset Your Device

First, reset the doorbell to factory settings. This will erase any unauthorized access and return the device to its original state.

Change Your Password Immediately

Once the device is reset, change your password to something stronger and unique. If you have 2FA enabled, ensure you update your second factor as well.

Contact Customer Support

If you notice suspicious activity that you can’t fix on your own, contact the doorbell’s manufacturer for assistance. They may be able to provide additional help or even detect and block unauthorized access.

Check for Breached Data

If your smart doorbell was compromised, there’s a chance that personal information like your Wi-Fi password or video footage could have been accessed. Take steps to change any sensitive information and secure your accounts.

Ongoing Security Practices for Your Smart Doorbell

Securing your smart doorbell isn’t a one-time thing—it requires regular attention. Follow the steps above, and make it a habit to check for updates, monitor your device activity, and stay informed about any new security threats. Consistent vigilance can keep your home safer from hackers.

For even more home security tips, consider securing other devices around your home, such as your TV or smart appliances. Secure your TV from hackers by following similar security steps for connected devices.

Conclusion

Smart doorbells are a convenient and valuable part of your smart home system, but they also require proper security measures. By following these steps to update firmware, set strong passwords, and secure your Wi-Fi, you can protect your doorbell from being hacked and ensure your home stays safe.
